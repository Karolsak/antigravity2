Problem Statement

A 3-phase induction motor has a 4-pole, star-connected stator winding. The motor runs on a line voltage of 400 V, 50 Hz supply. The motor resistance and standstill reactance per phase are 0.1 Ω and 1 Ω respectively. The ratio of stator to rotor turns is 4. Calculate: (i) Starting torque; (ii) Full load torque at slip = 4%; (iii) Slip at maximum torque and rotor speed; (iv) Maximum torque; (v) Ratio T_st/T_max and T_fl/T_max; (vi) Additional rotor resistance for max torque at start.

Synchronous speed: N_s = 120f/p = 1500 rpm → ω_s = 2π×1500/60 = 157.08 rad/s

Torque formula (simplified equiv. circuit, stator Z neglected):

T = (3/ω_s) × V₁² × (R₂/s) / [(R₂/s)² + X₂²]

(i) Starting Torque (s = 1)

T_st = (3/ω_s) × V₁² × R₂ / (R₂² + X₂²)
T_st = (3/157.08) × (230.94)² × 0.1 / (0.01 + 1)
T_st = 0.01909 × 53333 × 0.1 / 1.01
✅ T_st = 100.85 N·m

(ii) Full Load Torque (s = 0.04)

R₂/s = 0.1/0.04 = 2.5 Ω
T_fl = (3/157.08) × (230.94)² × 2.5 / (2.5² + 1²)
T_fl = 0.01909 × 53333 × 2.5 / 7.25
✅ T_fl = 351.24 N·m

(iii) Slip & Speed at Max Torque

s_m = R₂/X₂ = 0.1/1.0 = 0.1 (10%)
N_maxT = N_s×(1 - s_m) = 1500×0.9
✅ s_m = 0.10 (10%) | N = 1350 rpm

(iv) Maximum Torque

T_max = (3/ω_s) × V₁² / (2×X₂)
T_max = (3/157.08) × (230.94)² / (2×1.0)
✅ T_max = 509.30 N·m

(v) Torque Ratios

T_st/T_max = 100.85/509.30 = 0.198
T_fl/T_max = 351.24/509.30 = 0.690
✅ T_st/T_max = 0.198 (19.8%)
✅ T_fl/T_max = 0.690 (69.0%)

(vi) Additional Rotor Resistance

For max torque at start: R₂_total = X₂ = 1.0 Ω (stator-referred)
R_add (stator-referred) = X₂ - R₂ = 1.0 - 0.1 = 0.9 Ω
R_add (actual rotor) = 0.9/(N₁/N₂)² = 0.9/16
✅ R_add = 0.05625 Ω per phase (actual rotor)
